!Series_summary	"Lymphatic dysfunction exists in tumor necrosis factor transgenic (TNF-Tg) mice and rheumatoid arthritis (RA) patients. While joint-draining TNF-Tg popliteal lymphatic vessels (PLVs) have deficits in contractility during end-stage arthritis, the nature of lymphatic muscle cells (LMCs) and their TNF altered transcriptome remain unknown. Thus, we performed single-cell RNA-sequencing (scRNAseq) on TNF-Tg vs wild-type peripheral vasculature. We utilized strategic enrichment for smooth muscle cells using a Cspg4-Cre;tdTomato reporter model, and through post-hoc analysis we validated the ability to enrich for these tdTomato+ populations based on scatter alone. As such, we evaluated changes in LMC populations vs vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs), and dynamic changes in peri-vascular immune cell populations during chronic inflammatory-erosive arthritis."
!Series_overall_design	"Four total single-cell RNA-seq samples are provided. Two samples are replicates from Cspg4-Cre;tdTomato mice with cell enrichment for tdTomato+ cells (tdT1 and tdT2). Two samples derive from age-matched (9-months-old) WT and TNF-Tg, where the WT serves as control to the TNF-Tg mice with chronic inflammatory-erosive arthritis. The WT and TNF-Tg samples were sorted based on scatter alone. Each sample consists of cell isolates derived from n = 3 mice pooled together, and all mice were on a C57BL6 background."

!Sample_extract_protocol_ch1	"Evan's blue dye was administered intradermal into the mouse hindpaw to identify the two popliteal lymphatic vessels that run on either side of the superficial saphenous vein in the back of the calf draining into the popliteal lymph node behind the knee. All three vessels were dissected, manually and enzymatically digested (Accumax; Innovative Cell Technologies Cat# AM-105) for 1h at room temperature, filtered with a 75um strainer, and single cell suspensions were sorted by fluorescence activated cell sorting based on tdTomato fluoresence (tdT1 & tdT2) or scatter profile (WT and TNF). Sorted cells were then submitted to the Genomics Research Center at the University of Rochester for library preparation and sequencing. All cells in each sample were loaded on a Chromium Single-Cell Insrument (10x Genomics) and single-cell Gel Bead-in-Emulsions were generated. The Chromium Single-Cell 3' Library & Gel Bead Kit (10x Genomics) was used to generate the library based on the manufacturer's instructions. Barcoded cDNA from the Gel Bead-in-Emulsions was purified with DynaBeads MyOne Silane Beads (ThermoFisher Scientific Cat# PN37002D) with PCR amplification for 3' cDNA library construction. NextSeq 550 (tdT1) and NovaSeq 6000 (tdT2, WT, TNF) Illumina sequencers generated the sequenced data."
